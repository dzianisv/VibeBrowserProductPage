## Daily Posting Routine (30 min)

### Step 1: Reply first (15 min)
Before posting anything, reply to 5–10 accounts in your niche. This is the single highest-ROI action.

**Target accounts to engage with:**
- `@AnthropicAI` / `@ClaudeAI` threads about Claude Code
- `@OpenAI` / `@sama` threads about agents
- MCP ecosystem builders (`@modelcontextprotocol`)
- Browser automation accounts
- Developer tools founders building in public

**Reply rules:**
- Add something new — a counterpoint, a deeper detail, a related observation
- Never generic ("Great point!", "Totally agree") — that's noise
- Don't mention VibeBrowser in replies for the first 2 weeks
- One sharp sentence beats five rambling ones

### Step 2: Post content (10 min)
Pick one content pillar. Write it. Post it.

**Quick formats that work:**
- Single sharp observation (1 tweet): "The auth re-auth loop is the most underrated pain in AI agent dev."
- Short tip (1 tweet): "Quick tip: `npx @vibebrowser/mcp` gives Claude Code your real browser cookies in 30s. No Browserbase account."
- Thread (5–8 tweets): hook → expand → solution → CTA

### Step 3: Engage with replies (5 min)
Reply to everyone who replies to your posts within the first hour. Early engagement signals boost algorithmic reach significantly.

## Thread Mastery

Threads are the highest-leverage format on X. A single great thread can bring 500–2000 followers.

### Thread structure (always use this)

```
Tweet 1 — Hook: counterintuitive claim or sharp pain point
Tweet 2 — Expand the problem (why it matters, who it affects)
Tweet 3 — The common solution and why it fails
Tweet 4 — "So I built X" — introduce VibeBrowser
Tweet 5 — How it works (1 technical detail)
Tweet 6 — The key differentiator (the insight)
Tweet 7 — Social proof or metric
Tweet 8 — CTA: try it free / link / follow for more
```

### Hook tweet formulas (steal these)

**Counterintuitive:**
> "You're paying Browserbase $150/mo for a feature you don't need."

**Observation:**
> "Every AI agent tutorial shows you a fresh anonymous browser. That's the wrong mental model for 80% of use cases."

**The math:**
> "Browserbase charges per browser-minute. Health check pings alone cost me $40/mo. I was paying to keep an empty browser alive."

**The insight:**
> "Browserbase solves the 'I'm a bot pretending to be human' problem. But if you're automating your own Gmail — you're not a bot. Why pay for stealth?"

**The before/after:**
> "Before: Give AI agent browser access = 2 days of Playwright auth session management. After: `npx @vibebrowser/mcp`. 30 seconds."

### Thread best practices

- **Tweet 1 is everything.** If the hook doesn't make someone stop scrolling, nothing else matters.
- **One idea per tweet.** Break aggressively. Short sentences. Line breaks.
- **Number your threads** — "1/" at the end of tweet 1 signals a thread is coming.
- **End with a call to follow** — "Follow me for more on browser automation for AI agents."
- **Screenshot > link** — embed screenshots of the product rather than linking to it mid-thread.
- **Post between 8–11am US Eastern on weekdays.** That's when tech Twitter is active.

## Build in Public (highest-trust content)

"Build in public" content gets disproportionate engagement because it's rare and authentic.

**What to share:**
- Waitlist milestones: "50 signups → 100 signups → 500 signups"
- Weekly active user count (once you have it)
- Conversion rate of the waitlist page
- Technical decisions: "We chose CDP over Playwright because…"
- Failures: "We tried X, it didn't work, here's why"
- Learnings from user interviews
- Revenue (when relevant)

**Format:**
```
We just hit [metric].

Here's exactly what drove it:
- [thing 1]
- [thing 2]
- [thing 3]

The surprising one: [thing 3 detail]

[optional: screenshot or graph]
```

This format gets saved, bookmarked, and shared. It also signals that you're real and the product is real.

## Threads (Meta) Strategy

Threads is growing but different from X:

**What's different:**
- Less link-friendly — Threads deprioritizes posts with external URLs; share links in replies or bio
- More casual tone — write like you're texting a smart friend, not presenting at a conference
- No hashtags (they don't meaningfully boost reach on Threads)
- Longer form text performs better than on X
- Repost + comment ("repost with note") is the main amplification mechanism

**What to post on Threads:**
- Casual observations about building ("Today I found out Browserbase charges for idle browsers. Wild.")
- Behind-the-scenes product building (same build-in-public content, shorter)
- Cross-post your X threads (rewrite for Threads tone — remove numbers, make it more conversational)
- User feedback highlights ("Someone just automated their entire onboarding process with VibeBrowser")

**Threads growth tactics:**
- Reply to tech founders and AI builders who are active on Threads
- Repost with commentary (adds your take to someone else's post — your followers see it + their followers may too)
- Post at different times than X — Threads audience is often more international and evening-active

## What NOT to Do

```
❌ Don't post promotional links without context ("Check out VibeBrowser!")
❌ Don't follow/unfollow to game follower counts — ruins your engagement rate
❌ Don't buy followers — destroys algorithmic reach
❌ Don't post the same content at the same time every day (algorithm penalizes mechanical patterns)
❌ Don't ignore replies — responding is the #1 signal to the algorithm that your content is engaging
❌ Don't only post about your product — mix in genuine opinions on the space
❌ Don't delete and repost for better timing — just accept the post and move on
❌ Don't use throwaway accounts for any promotion (see anti-ban.md)
```
