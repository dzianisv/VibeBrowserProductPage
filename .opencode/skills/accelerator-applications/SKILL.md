# Accelerator Application Expert Knowledge

Use this skill when preparing applications for Y Combinator, a16z Speedrun, or other startup accelerators.

## a16z Speedrun Application Insights

Based on SR006 review (19,000+ applications, <0.4% acceptance):

### What Sets Founders Apart

1. **Co-founder relationships with depth**
   - Teams that really know each other: college roommates, former coworkers, high school coding partners
   - Shared history creates sustainability and deeper context
   - New pairings CAN work, but prioritize people you have reps with

2. **Velocity over just traction**
   - How fast are you developing, releasing, and learning?
   - Not all traction is equal - revenue > design partners > influencer advisors
   - Talk to potential customers, find design partners for rough products

3. **The value of ideas**
   - Ideas matter more than people think
   - How did you come to this idea? Why is this important to you?
   - What's your earned insight? What secret have you learned?
   - Avoid "tar pits" - obvious ideas that lots of teams see

4. **Validation over small traction**
   - Strong validation without a product can beat weak traction with one
   - Talk to a LARGE number of customers
   - Look for: customers excited, introducing you to friends, talking about you

5. **Know the industry history**
   - Why did certain companies succeed or fail?
   - What was tried in the past?
   - Map out and talk to all players in your industry

### Application Format & Style

- **Deck**: 6-10 slides MAX (some say 7)
- **Large font** on slides
- **SCQA format**: Situation, Complication, Question, Answer
- **Bottom line up front** - most important stuff in first 3 sentences
- **Updated LinkedIn profiles** - this IS part of your application
- **Brevity is clarity** - if you can't say it concisely, you don't understand it

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Not talking about the team enough** - people get enamored with product
2. **Too much time on product without the "why"** - don't just walk through a demo
3. **Overblown TAM** - don't claim every person wants your product
4. **Not highlighting differentiation** - make it clear vs competitors
5. **Burying the lead** - put great news (ARR, design partners) front and center
6. **Typos** - shows poor attention to detail
7. **Rambling** - tighten it up, use AI to edit for conciseness

### Interview Best Practices

1. **Think movie trailer** - 10 minutes to convince for a longer meeting
2. **Articulate vision at different levels** - one-liner to deep dive
3. **Brag about yourself** - build conviction you'll beat 50 other teams
4. **Show founder chemistry** - clear roles, no awkward silences
5. **Demonstrate you're a student of company building** - not just product obsessed

### What Makes Founders Stand Out

1. **Signals of exceptionality** - Outlier things beyond fancy schools
2. **Done the work** - Talked to customers, know industry history, met key players
3. **Truth-seekers** - Changed their mind, learned from failures
4. **Can articulate vision clearly** - Like a movie trailer in 10 minutes
5. **Strong online presence** - LinkedIn, Twitter, YouTube with real insights

### For "Outsider" Founders

Show you've done the work:
- Know the history of the market
- Understand why competitors failed
- Met players across the industry
- Have insider perspectives from research

## Vibe Browser - Application Content

### One-sentence description

Vibe Browser is an AI browser agent that autonomously executes web tasks - research, outreach, form filling - using any AI model while running 100% private on your device.

### What are you building?

An autonomous browser co-pilot that doesn't just answer questions - it acts. Users describe tasks in plain English and Vibe executes multi-step workflows across any website.

**What makes us different:**

1. **True local AI**: Gemini Nano runs entirely on-device. Your browsing data never leaves your machine.
2. **Model freedom**: GPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok, DeepSeek - switch anytime. No vendor lock-in.
3. **Chrome extension**: Works on any Chrome browser. Competitors require browser forks.
4. **User stays in control**: Every action is visible. Critical actions require approval.

### Why are you the right team?

**Dzianis Vashchuk (Founder)** built exactly this at Coinbase. For 3.5 years, developed LLM agent tooling for on-call engineers - integrating AI with Databricks, Datadog, GitHub, and Jira. Before that, shipped distributed systems at BitTorrent.

The insight came from watching engineers spend hours on repetitive web tasks that required human judgment but not human clicking.

### Unique market insight

**The secret**: Browser AI agents will be commoditized by whoever wins the privacy battle.

Everyone is building cloud-based agents because it's easier. But the best browser automation requires accessing logged-in accounts - Gmail, LinkedIn, bank accounts. Users won't give that access to a cloud service.

Chrome's Gemini Nano (shipped 2024) changes everything. For the first time, capable AI runs 100% locally in the browser.

**Market validation:**
- Manus: $100M+ ARR in <1 year, acquired by Meta for $2B
- OpenAI Operator: Launched as flagship product
- Perplexity Comet: Major investment

But all are cloud-only, single-model. The privacy-first, model-agnostic player hasn't emerged yet.

### What we've learned from users (50+ conversations)

1. **Privacy is non-negotiable for power users** - compliance kills cloud-based tools
2. **Model preference varies wildly** - forcing one model kills adoption
3. **The last mile is approval, not execution** - users want to review before sending
4. **Existing tools break constantly** - LinkedIn automation fails after every update

### Traction

- **Live product**: Released on Chrome Web Store
- **Paying users**: Early customers through direct outreach
- **Waitlist**: ~300 signups at vibebrowser.app
- **Demo workflows**: LinkedIn outreach, Gmail management, Calendar automation
- **Burn**: $5-10k/month (bootstrapped)

### 3-month roadmap

**Month 1: Reliability** - Agent success rate 70% -> 90%, better error recovery
**Month 2: Growth** - Workflow templates, user-shared library, Chrome Web Store optimization
**Month 3: Monetization** - Pro tier launch, usage-based pricing, team features

**Key metric**: 100 active weekly users by end of program

### How we make money

- **Free**: Unlimited local AI (Gemini Nano), basic cloud models
- **Pro ($25/mo)**: Premium cloud models (GPT-5.1, Claude, Grok-4)
- **Max ($99/mo)**: Full reasoning models (GPT-5.2, DeepSeek-R1)

Unit economics: Cloud AI costs ~$0.01/query. At $25/month for ~100 queries, 3x+ margin.

### Why we win vs competitors

1. **Privacy moat**: Enterprises with compliance requirements can't use cloud-only solutions
2. **Platform approach**: AI-agnostic infrastructure. As models commoditize, we become more valuable
3. **Distribution**: Chrome extension = instant install in existing browser. No download, no friction
