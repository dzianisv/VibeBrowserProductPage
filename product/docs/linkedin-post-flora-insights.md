# LinkedIn Post: The AI Browser That Gets Power Users

## Version 1: Story-Driven (Recommended)

---

**Silicon Valley keeps building the wrong AI tools.**

I just read an a16z interview with Weber Wong, founder of FLORA (raised $42M for creative AI tools). His insight hit home:

"Silicon Valley has completely misunderstood what professionals actually need from AI."

His point: Engineers build "prompt in, magic out" tools. But professionals don't want magic. They want *control*.

- They want to see every step
- They want to approve before execution  
- They want repeatable workflows they can trust

This is exactly why we built Vibe Browser differently.

Not "let AI do it for you."
But "a co-pilot that assists, not takes over."

The best AI tools don't replace expertise—they amplify it.

What's your take: Do you want more control from AI tools, or more automation?

---

## Version 2: Insight-Focused (Shorter)

---

Most AI tools are built backwards.

Engineers think: "How do I automate this task?"
Professionals think: "How do I stay in control while saving time?"

That gap is why so many AI products feel like toys, not tools.

The fix is simple:
→ Show every step
→ Require approval before action
→ Build for repeatability

AI should amplify expertise, not replace judgment.

That's the difference between magic tricks and real leverage.

## Version 3: Hot Take (Engagement-Optimized)

---

Unpopular opinion: "Fully autonomous AI" is bad product design.

Here's why the best professionals don't want it:

❌ Black box = anxiety
❌ One-shot = no iteration
❌ No visibility = no trust

What they actually want:

✅ See every step
✅ Approve every action
✅ Repeatable workflows

AI that replaces you is a threat.
AI that amplifies you is a superpower.

Building for control > building for magic.

## Version 4: Quote-Led (Thought Leadership)

---

"The value of taste will actually go up."

This quote from Weber Wong (FLORA, $42M Series A) changed how I think about AI tools.

His insight: When AI handles the rote work, what matters is *judgment*.

The professionals who thrive with AI will be those who:
• Know what good looks like
• Can course-correct in real-time
• Build systems they can trust

AI doesn't replace power users. It extends their reach.

The bottleneck was always time. Now it's taste.
